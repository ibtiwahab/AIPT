Instructions

Begin by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing your body. Keep a slight bend in your elbows. Engage your core muscles and maintain a neutral spine. Start the movement by simultaneously lifting both dumbbells out to your sides, away from your body. Keep your arms straight or with a slight bend in your elbows. Continue lifting the dumbbells until your arms are parallel to the ground or slightly below shoulder level. Avoid swinging or using momentum to lift the weights. Pause briefly at the top position, feeling the contraction in your shoulder muscles. Keep your shoulders down and away from your ears. Slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position, maintaining control and resisting gravity. Repeat the lateral raise motion, focusing on proper form and avoiding any excessive swinging or shrugging of your shoulders. Gradually increase the weight or number of repetitions as your strength improves.
